# Computational Notebooks for Physics and Mathematics

This repository contains a collection of Jupyter notebooks designed to support the teaching and understanding of concepts in physics and mathematics.

The notebooks are intended to complement traditional lectures by providing step-by-step computational demonstrations, visualizations, and controlled experiments. The emphasis is on clarity, intuition, and reproducibility.

---

## Purpose

Many mathematical and physical concepts are introduced in an abstract form. These notebooks aim to make such concepts more accessible by:

- translating equations into computational procedures  
- visualizing intermediate and final results  
- allowing controlled modification of parameters  
- connecting formal definitions with observable behavior  

The material is suitable for undergraduate-level courses and for self-study.

---

## Current Content

The repository currently includes:

- Fourier analysis applied to images  
  - representation of images in frequency space  
  - filtering and reconstruction  
  - comparison of structured and random frequency selection  
  - role of magnitude and phase  

Additional notebooks will be added over time.
